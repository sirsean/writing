Saving the closer for the 9th inning might have value even if he'd be better served facing the 3-4-5 in the 8th.

The 9th inning is when rallies happen, when magic pokes its head into the game. Having the psychological advantage of the super-fireman waiting in the closer role has to help, to prevent that magic from happening.

Can you measure it? Probably not.

Can you believe in it? Sure you can.
